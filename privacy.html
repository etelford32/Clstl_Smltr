1. Controller & Scope

The data controller responsible for your personal data under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, and equivalent laws is:

  • Telford Projects LLC, a U.S. limited liability company doing business as Parkers Physics App.
  • Privacy contact: privacy@parkersphysics.com
  • Postal contact: available on request via the privacy email above.

This Policy applies to the Parkers Physics App website at parkersphysics.com, any installable Progressive Web App built from it, our public and authenticated APIs, and all interactive simulations and data feeds provided therein (collectively, the "Service"). It does not apply to third-party sites we link to, which have their own privacy policies.

Where this Policy refers to "personal data" or "personal information," those terms have the meanings given in the GDPR/UK GDPR and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), respectively.

2. Data We Collect

We practice data minimization: we collect only what is necessary to operate, secure, and improve the Service. The personal data we may process falls into the following categories.

  • Identifiers & Account Data (CCPA cat. A) — name (or display name), email address, salted+hashed password, account ID, optional institution / affiliation.
  • Customer Records (CCPA cat. B) — billing name, billing country, last 4 digits of payment card and card brand (received from Stripe; we never see the full PAN), subscription tier and history.
  • Commercial Information (CCPA cat. D) — products / plans purchased, transaction timestamps, refund history.
  • Internet / Network Activity (CCPA cat. F) — pages visited within the Service, simulations launched, feature interactions, click events, error and crash reports, request timestamps, referral URL.
  • Geolocation (coarse only) (CCPA cat. G) — country and approximate region inferred from IP address, used for compliance routing (e.g., GDPR vs. CCPA experiences) and abuse prevention. We do not collect precise device geolocation.
  • Device & Technical Data — IP address, browser type and version, operating system, viewport / screen resolution, device type, language, time zone.
  • User-Provided Content — saved simulation parameters, named locations, dashboard configurations, support correspondence, feedback you submit.
  • API & Developer Data — API key identifiers (we store hashes of full keys, not the keys themselves), the labels and intended-use descriptions you provide when issuing a key, request logs (timestamp, source IP, endpoint, status code, response size), and aggregate usage counters used for billing and rate-limit enforcement. See Section 6 for detail.
  • Inferences (CCPA cat. K) — limited operational inferences such as "active in the last 30 days" or "uses launch-planner feature regularly," used to prioritize features and improve the Service.

We do not collect biometric data, government identifiers, precise geolocation, sensitive characteristics protected by anti-discrimination law, content of private communications other than what you send us directly, or social-media credentials. We do not knowingly process "special category" personal data within the meaning of GDPR Article 9.

We use personal data only for the purposes set out below. We do not use your data to serve third-party advertisements, and we do not engage in cross-context behavioral advertising.

  • Authenticate you and maintain your signed-in session

  • Process payments and issue receipts (via Stripe)
  • Send transactional emails: account confirmation, password reset, security alerts, billing receipts, plan changes, service-impacting incidents
  • Send optional product updates only where you have opted in (or for U.S. recipients on a CAN-SPAM compliant basis you can unsubscribe from at any time)
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, account takeover, denial-of-service, scraping, and other security incidents
  • Diagnose errors, monitor performance, and improve simulations, data feeds, and user interfaces
  • Compute aggregate, de-identified statistics about usage of the Service
  • Comply with our legal obligations and enforce our EULA

4. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, we rely on the following legal bases under Article 6 GDPR / UK GDPR:

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) — to create and operate your account, deliver features included in your plan, process payments, and provide customer support.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — to keep the Service secure and reliable, prevent abuse and fraud, debug errors, analyze usage in aggregate, and improve our scientific simulations and data products. We balance these interests against your rights and freedoms; you may object at any time (see Section 13).
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — for non-essential cookies and similar technologies, and for any optional marketing communications. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)) — to retain transaction and tax records, respond to lawful requests from authorities, and meet other obligations imposed by law.

5. Third-Party Services & Sub-Processors

We rely on a small number of trusted vendors who act as processors (in GDPR terms) or service providers / contractors (in CCPA terms). They process personal data only on our documented instructions and under written contracts that include confidentiality, security, sub-processor, and data-deletion obligations.

  • Supabase Inc. — managed Postgres database, authentication, and storage. Hosts account records, hashed passwords, saved configurations. Privacy Policy.

  • Transactional email provider (e.g., Resend / Postmark / equivalent) — delivers account, security, and billing emails.

  • NASA DONKI — CME and geomagnetic storm catalog (api.nasa.gov)
  • NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC) — solar wind, Kp index, X-ray flux, geomagnetic alerts (services.swpc.noaa.gov)

  • NASA / ESA SOHO, STEREO, SDO — solar imagery
  • National Weather Service — terrestrial alerts (api.weather.gov)
  • CelesTrak and similar public catalogs — orbital elements (TLEs)

An up-to-date list of sub-processors is available on request to privacy@parkersphysics.com and is referenced in our Data Processing Agreement.

6. API Use & Authenticated API Access

This Section explains how we handle personal data when you (or systems acting on your behalf) use our HTTP REST API, WebSocket streams, or embeddable widgets (the "API"). Use of the API is also subject to Section 7 of our EULA and our API & Data Policy.

6a. What we collect when you use the API

  • API key metadata — a public key identifier, a salted hash of the secret portion (we cannot recover the secret after creation), the issuing account, the label and intended-use description you provided, the plan tier, and the creation / last-rotated / revoked timestamps.
  • Request logs — timestamp, source IP address, HTTP method, endpoint path, query-parameter shape (not values containing personal data), response status code, response size in bytes, latency, and a coarse client identifier (such as the User-Agent or a derived fingerprint hash) used for abuse detection.
  • Usage counters — per-key and per-account request totals, used to enforce rate limits and to bill metered plans.
  • Error and security events — authentication failures, rate-limit hits, anomaly-detection signals.

We do not log full request bodies by default. We may sample requests for debugging with appropriate access controls, and only when necessary to investigate a specific incident or to fix a defect. We do not log Authorization headers or other secrets.

6b. Why we process this data & legal bases

  • Provide and bill the API (contract — GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Enforce rate limits, detect abuse, prevent fraud, secure the platform (legitimate interests — Art. 6(1)(f)).
  • Debug defects and improve reliability (legitimate interests).
  • Meet legal, tax, and audit obligations (legal obligation — Art. 6(1)(c)).

6c. Outbound calls to upstream scientific data sources

When the Service or API serves you data sourced from upstream public APIs (NOAA SWPC, NASA DONKI, JPL Horizons, NWS, CelesTrak, etc.), our backend makes the upstream request on your behalf. We do not forward your account identifiers, IP address, or API key to those upstream sources. Where we serve cached responses, no upstream call is made at all.

6d. Embedding and developer use cases

If you embed our widgets or render our data on a site you operate, the site visitor's browser will exchange standard HTTP metadata (IP, User-Agent, Referer header) with our infrastructure to deliver the embed. You are responsible for disclosing this in your own site's privacy notice.

6e. Retention specific to the API

  • API key metadata — retained while the key is active, plus 12 months after revocation for fraud and audit purposes.
  • API request logs — full-fidelity logs retained up to 90 days; aggregated counters and security event records retained up to 13 months.
  • Billing-relevant usage records — retained for up to 7 years to satisfy U.S. tax requirements.

6f. Your control

You can rotate or revoke API keys at any time from your developer dashboard. Deleting your account also revokes all associated keys. You may request export of API key metadata and aggregated usage counters under Sections 13 and 14.

7. Cookies & Local Storage

We classify the cookies and similar technologies we use as follows. EU/EEA, UK, and Swiss visitors are presented with a consent banner on first visit; non-essential categories are off by default until you opt in.

  • Strictly necessary (always on): authentication session, CSRF token, load balancing, consent-state cookie. These are required to operate the Service and cannot be disabled.
  • Functional / preference (consent-based outside the U.S.): saved UI settings, simulation parameters, named locations. Stored in browser localStorage on your device.
  • Analytics (consent-based outside the U.S.): privacy-respecting, first-party analytics about page views and feature usage. We do not use ad-tech analytics.
  • No advertising or cross-site tracking cookies are set by us.

You can change your choices at any time using the Cookie Settings link in our footer, or by clearing site data in your browser. Choices are stored locally on your device, so they may need to be re-set on each browser you use.

10. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as needed for the purposes described in this Policy or as required by law. Specific retention windows include:

  • Account data — for the life of your account; deleted within 30 days of a verified deletion request, except where retention is required by law.
  • Authentication and security logs — up to 12 months from the event.
  • Aggregate usage / analytics logs — up to 24 months, then deleted or irreversibly aggregated.
  • Billing and tax records — up to 7 years following the transaction, as required by U.S. tax law.
  • Support correspondence — up to 3 years from the last exchange.
  • Backups — encrypted backups are retained on a rolling basis (typically 30 days) before being overwritten; deletion requests are honored in production immediately and propagate through backup rotation.
  • API key metadata, request logs, and usage counters — see Section 6e for the API-specific retention table.

11. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including:

  • HTTPS / TLS for all traffic between your browser and the Service
  • Encryption at rest for the production database and backups
  • Salted password hashing using a memory-hard algorithm
  • Row-Level Security policies in our database to enforce per-user access
  • Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
  • Principle of least privilege for staff and vendor access
  • Application-level rate limiting and abuse detection
  • Monitoring, logging, and dependency vulnerability scanning

No system is perfectly secure. If you suspect unauthorized access to your account or believe you have discovered a security vulnerability, please contact security@parkersphysics.com. Where required by law, we will notify affected users and supervisory authorities of personal-data breaches without undue delay (and within 72 hours under GDPR Article 33 where feasible).

13. Your Rights (GDPR & UK GDPR)

If you are in the EEA, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the GDPR and UK GDPR. Exercising these rights is free and we will respond within one month (extendable by two further months for complex requests, with notice).

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — obtain confirmation of whether we process your personal data and a copy of it.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure / "to be forgotten" (Art. 17) — request deletion in defined circumstances.
  • Right to restrict processing (Art. 18).
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20) — receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — object to processing based on our legitimate interests, including profiling.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) — at any time, without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects (Art. 22) — see Section 16.
  • Right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g., the Irish DPC, the UK ICO, the Swiss FDPIC) or with the authority of your habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement.

To exercise any right, email privacy@parkersphysics.com. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act:

  • Right to know the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, the sources, the business purposes, and the categories of recipients.
  • Right to delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to statutory exceptions.
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing of personal information. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, so no opt-out is operationally required, but you may confirm in writing.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information. We do not collect sensitive personal information as defined by the CPRA beyond log-in credentials necessary to access your account, which we use only to authenticate you.
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising any of these rights.

Categories of personal information we have collected in the prior 12 months: identifiers (Cat. A); customer records (Cat. B); commercial information (Cat. D); internet/network activity (Cat. F); coarse geolocation (Cat. G); inferences (Cat. K). Categories disclosed for a business purpose: A, B, D, F to our sub-processors listed in Section 5. Categories sold or shared: none.

You may exercise these rights by emailing privacy@parkersphysics.com or by submitting an authorized agent's request with documentation of authority. We honor browser-level Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals as a valid opt-out preference.

"Shine the Light" (Cal. Civ. Code §1798.83): we do not share personal information with third parties for their direct marketing purposes.
