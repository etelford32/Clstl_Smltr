Physics Notes▼

Roche figure: surface = isopotential of Φgrav+Φcent. Solved iteratively per-vertex: r = 1 + (β/2)·r³·sin²θ in polar units. Surface normal taken from −∇Φ; the Roche-critical limit β = 8/27 ≈ 0.296 corresponds to ω/ωc = 1 (Req/Rp = 1.5).

Gravity darkening: classical von Zeipel '24 gives T(θ) ∝ geff(θ)β with β = 0.25 for radiative envelopes. Espinosa Lara & Rieutord '11 self-consistently solve the radiative-flux-conservation equation on the Roche surface and find βeff falls toward ~0.18 as ω → ωc; we use a fitted closed-form to their Fig. 4.

Planck colour: per-fragment T mapped to RGB via Tanner-Helland blackbody fit with seam-smoothing across 6600 K so gravity-darkened B-star equators don't band.

Limb darkening: Claret quadratic I(μ)/I(1) = 1 − a(1 − μ) − b(1 − μ)², a=0.32, b=0.28 (B6V V-band-ish).

Be viscous decretion disk (Lee, Saio & Osaki '91; Carciofi & Bjorkman '08): isothermal H II plasma in Keplerian rotation, ρ(r,z) = ρ₀ (Req/r)n·exp(−z²/2H²) with H(r) ∝ r1.5. We render the disk as a true 3D volume — front-to-back raymarched emission with τ-attenuation. Edge-on lines of sight pierce more plasma → strong Hα limb.

Doppler vlos map reproduces what optical/IR interferometers (AMBER, PIONIER, GRAVITY) measure as differential phase — directly maps Ω̂ × r⃗ projected on the line of sight.

Non-radial pulsation: the surface displacement δr ∝ Y22 ∝ sin²θ·cos(2φ − σt) is the prograde sectoral g-mode candidate driver of Achernar's episodic disk ejection (Vinicius+ '06, Goss+ '11).

Synthetic Hα profile: emission from the Keplerian disk is computed by integrating Σ(r)·δ(v − vK(r) sin i cos φ) over the disk; rotational broadening of the photospheric component uses the Gray uniform-disk profile. The V/R asymmetry is an m=1 disk-precession proxy.
