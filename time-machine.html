First-order linear extrapolation of Standish (1992) secular rates. Reproduces the modern apsidal-precession trend (~11.45″/yr in ω̄ inertial → ~21,000-yr climatic-precession period when combined with axial precession), but does not capture the ~100-kyr Milankovitch eccentricity cycle, which arises from second-order gravitational coupling with Venus and Jupiter and requires the full Wisdom-Holman tier.

Earth N-body − Linear-Secular Residuals — follows body picker

Run the N-body validation above, then pick a planet from the body picker beside the orrery to populate this table.

Residuals are computed at fixed sample epochs as Δ = (full N-body osculating element) − (Standish linear-secular value). Initial state is seeded from Standish at the centre epoch so all residuals start at zero — values then evolve as Yoshida-4 captures higher-order perturbative coupling that the linear model omits. Mercury and Neptune residuals are inflated by integrator phase error at dt = 1 d (Mercury's 88-day period is under-resolved; Neptune's near-circular orbit amplifies element noise).

Phase 1 — Validated Window Live

The orrery above is driven by the unified ephemeris engine that powers the Solar System and Space Weather pages. Inner planets use Meeus three-term Kepler with Standish secular rates; outer planets use VSOP87D truncated series. Sub-arcminute residuals across ±200 yr; degraded but useful out to ±2,000 yr. Beyond that band, results drift — the long-horizon Yoshida-4 and Wisdom-Holman tiers below are still in development.

Predictions over kyr–Myr scales are inherently uncertain — the inner solar system is chaotic with a Lyapunov time of ~5 Myr (Laskar 1989). Anything past ~60 Myr is statistical, not deterministic.

Wisdom-Holman map
Milankovitch & chaos
Mixed-variable symplectic mapping for million-year integrations. Earth-orbit eccentricity and obliquity cycles tied to climate forcing. Beyond the Lyapunov horizon results are presented as ensembles, not single trajectories.

What Will Live Here

A single workspace with a JD slider, a body picker, and three views — orbital-element plots over time, instantaneous 3D state, and close-approach tables. All three time horizons share the same backend; switching tier just changes integrator and step size.

The page will surface integrator diagnostics directly: energy drift, angular-momentum drift, residuals against VSOP87D inside its validity band, and Lyapunov estimates for chaos-bound runs.
