Orbital Time Machine

A deep-time workbench for solar-system orbital mechanics. Wind the planets thousands of years forward or millions of years back, watch perihelia precess, and study chaos in the inner system. This page is a placeholder while the underlying N-body engine is built — the live ±2,000 yr propagator on the Solar System and Space Weather pages comes first.

Under Construction

The orbital time machine will sit on top of the unified ephemeris engine being built for the Earth and Solar-System pages. Phase 1 ships a live ±2,000 yr Yoshida-4 symplectic integrator validated against VSOP87D and JPL Horizons. Once that baseline is stable and instrumented for collision search, this page becomes the long-horizon harness on top of it.

Predictions over kyr–Myr scales are inherently uncertain — the inner solar system is chaotic with a Lyapunov time of ~5 Myr (Laskar 1989). Anything past ~60 Myr is statistical, not deterministic.

Roadmap

±2,000 yr
VSOP87D + Yoshida-4
Live propagation (current focus)
Symplectic integrator seeded from VSOP87D state vectors at J2000. Sub-arcminute residuals across the working window. Powers the 3D orrery, collision search, and the Earth orbit panel. Lives in the existing js/ephemeris/ module — not on this page yet.
±10,000 yr
Yoshida-4 / Yoshida-8 N-body
Holocene & near-future
Same integrator as Phase 1 with a longer step and the major asteroids (Ceres, Vesta, Pallas, Hygiea) added as perturbers. Visualises Earth's apsidal precession (≈21,000 yr cycle) and the Jupiter-Saturn great inequality (~883 yr beat).
±1,000,000 yr
Wisdom-Holman map
Milankovitch & chaos
Mixed-variable symplectic mapping for million-year integrations. Runs Earth-orbit eccentricity and obliquity cycles tied to climate forcing. Beyond the Lyapunov horizon results are presented as ensembles, not single trajectories.

What Will Live Here

A single workspace with a JD slider, a body picker, and three views — orbital-element plots over time, instantaneous 3D state, and close-approach tables. All three time horizons share the same backend; switching tier just changes integrator and step size.

The page will surface integrator diagnostics directly: energy drift, angular-momentum drift, residuals against VSOP87D inside its validity band, and Lyapunov estimates for chaos-bound runs.
