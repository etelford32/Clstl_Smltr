6. Simulation Data, Accuracy & Best Intent

Parkers Physics App provides physics simulations and live space-weather data visualizations for educational, research, and informational purposes. We take the scientific integrity of our outputs seriously and apply the commitments in this section in good faith.

Simulation outputs are not certified scientific products. Do not use Parkers Physics App data for professional aerospace, aviation, emergency management, satellite operations, navigation, life-safety, or other safety-critical decision-making. Always consult official sources (NOAA SWPC, NASA, ESA, ICAO) for operational space-weather data.

6a. Data Provenance & Freshness

Live data feeds are sourced from publicly available scientific and government APIs. The primary upstream sources we rely on, together with their typical refresh cadence, are:

  • NOAA SWPC — solar wind plasma & magnetic field, Kp / planetary indices, GOES X-ray flux (1–5 minute cadence)
  • NASA DONKI — CME catalogs, geomagnetic storm and solar flare events (event-driven)
  • NASA / ESA SOHO, STEREO, SDO — coronagraph and solar imager frames (15–60 minute cadence)
  • JPL Horizons — planetary and small-body ephemerides (on-demand)
  • National Weather Service (NWS) & api.weather.gov — terrestrial space-weather alerts
  • CelesTrak and equivalent public TLE catalogs — orbital elements for satellite tracking

When upstream data is delayed, missing, or appears anomalous in ways we can detect, we will indicate that condition in the user interface (for example, a "stale data" or "feed unavailable" badge) on a best-effort basis. We do not modify upstream values; if a value is shown without such a badge, it represents the most recent observation we received from the named source.

6b. Best-Effort Accuracy Commitment

We use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that:

  • Live data values displayed to you are transmitted unmodified from the authoritative upstream source identified above, except for unit conversions and standard interpolations clearly disclosed in the user interface or documentation.
  • Simulations are implemented to follow published, peer-reviewed physics models and standards (for example, SGP4/SDP4 for orbital propagation, ideal-MHD formulations for solar-wind simulations, blackbody and Planck-function approximations for stellar models).
  • Numerical methods and approximations used by our simulators are documented to a level sufficient for an informed reader to evaluate their fitness for purpose.

This is a good-faith commitment, not a warranty. It does not modify the disclaimers in Sections 11 and 12.

6c. Model Limitations & Known Approximations

All numerical simulations are approximations. You acknowledge and accept the following well-known limitations of the models used by Parkers Physics App, among others:

  • SGP4/SDP4 orbital propagation accumulates error over time and degrades for objects with rapidly changing drag, recently maneuvered satellites, and decaying orbits. TLEs may be hours to days old.
  • MHD solar-wind simulations use simplified geometries, finite grid resolution, and idealized boundary conditions; they do not resolve kinetic-scale physics.
  • Stellar models use simplified opacity tables, parameterized convection, and one-dimensional approximations.
  • CME propagation models typically have arrival-time uncertainties on the order of several hours.
  • Image and field overlays may use reprojection or color mappings chosen for legibility rather than scientific quantitative analysis.

6d. Corrections & Errata

If we identify a material error in a simulator, data pipeline, or data display — or if such an error is reported to us and confirmed — we will endeavor in good faith to (i) correct the error in a reasonable timeframe; (ii) note the correction in a public changelog or release-notes page; and (iii) where the error materially affected paid users, notify affected account holders by email.
