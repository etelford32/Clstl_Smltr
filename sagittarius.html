RIAF/MAD accretion — Sgr A*'s flow is a geometrically thick torus (H/R~1), NOT a thin disk. The EHT (2022) resolved a bright crescent at ~5Rg from Doppler beaming of the approaching side. Two-temperature plasma: Ti~10¹² K, Te~10&sup9;–¹&sup0; K. Emission is synchrotron (submm peak at 230 GHz), not blackbody. Likely in MAD state (Magnetically Arrested Disk) — poloidal B-field flux eruptions may trigger the observed flares. ADIOS wind: only ~1% of captured gas reaches the horizon (Blandford & Begelman 1999). Most is ejected in thermal outflows. Hot-spots: GRAVITY (2018) detected a NIR-bright blob orbiting near ISCO at ~45 min period.
